# Presentations, Email, and Social

## Presentations

### Slide types

1. **Title slide** — Dark Blue background, white text, white logo, Burst decoration (one corner)
2. **Content slide** — White background, Dark Blue headings, Dark Text body
3. **Section divider** — Dark Blue background, large white text, Orange accent line
4. **Image slide** — Full-bleed photo with dark-blue bottom gradient and white text overlay
5. **Data slide** — White background, charts in brand colors (see `web-digital.md`)
6. **Quote slide** — Dark Blue background, large Light text, Orange opening quotation mark as emphasis
7. **Closing slide** — Dark Blue background, white logo centered with tagline, optional Pulse in a corner

Use one layout per purpose — don't invent new slide backgrounds for variety.

### Layout rules

- Title on every non-divider slide, Dark Blue bold, left-aligned
- Body: bullet points, max 5 per slide. If you have more, split into two slides.
- Fonts: Helvetica Neue (or Arial on systems without it)
- Footer: small muted line with page number — either left ("TenneT") or right (`X / Y`)
- Logo: top-right on content slides (small, ~5% of width), or centered/large on title and closing slides
- Never use a photo background behind text without a gradient or dark overlay

### Typical slide count

A 30-minute presentation: 15–25 slides. Don't try to fit everything; cut slides, use the time for speaking.

## Email templates

### Email signature

```
Firstname Lastname
Job Title | Department
T +31 (0)26 xxx xxxx | M +31 (0)6 xxxx xxxx

[TenneT Logo]
TenneT TSO B.V. | tennet.eu
```

For German colleagues: `TenneT TSO GmbH | tennet.eu`.

### Email template colors

- Header bar: Dark Blue with white logo
- Body: White background, Dark Text (never fully styled HTML emails that rely on custom fonts — Helvetica Neue falls back to Arial on Outlook which is fine)
- Links: Bright Blue, underlined
- CTA button: Orange with white text, 12–16px padding, 4–6px border radius
- Footer: Near White background, Muted Text, legal/unsubscribe links smaller

### Subject lines

- Clear and direct — no "Re:" prefixes on cold mails
- Avoid all-caps and excessive punctuation
- Include the specific ask or topic in the first 40 characters

## Social media

### Image formats

| Platform | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post | 1200 × 627 | Landscape, branded template |
| LinkedIn banner | 1584 × 396 | Panoramic, logo right |
| Instagram post | 1080 × 1080 | Square, bold text overlay |
| Instagram story | 1080 × 1920 | Portrait, photo + gradient |
| X / Twitter post | 1200 × 675 | Similar to LinkedIn |
| YouTube thumbnail | 1280 × 720 | Similar ratio to LinkedIn |

### Rules

- Always include the TenneT logo
- Use Pulse/Burst as background texture — very subtle, 10–15% opacity (lower than poster use)
- Text overlay on images: max ~6 words for impact
- Use brand colors for any graphics, charts, icons
- Photo style: authentic, real infrastructure or people (see `../SKILL.md` § photography)
- No vertical banner text or rotated captions — TenneT visuals read horizontally
- Hashtags: TenneT's public channels use `#TenneT` and topic tags; don't invent fake ones in generated content

### Tone adjustments by platform

- **LinkedIn:** professional, data-driven, career-adjacent. Fine to include a link.
- **Instagram:** more visual, less text. Avoid long captions. Hashtags moved to a comment, not the main caption.
- **X / Twitter:** concise. Link previews work, long threads don't.

When in doubt about a platform-specific convention, keep the visual strict to brand and let the copy flex.
