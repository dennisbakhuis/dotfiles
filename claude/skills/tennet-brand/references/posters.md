# Posters

Five TenneT poster patterns, print sizes, and photo treatment.

## Print sizes

| Format | mm | px @ 300dpi |
|---|---|---|
| A2 Portrait | 420 × 594 | 4961 × 7016 |
| A2 Landscape | 594 × 420 | 7016 × 4961 |
| A3 Portrait | 297 × 420 | 3508 × 4961 |
| A3 Landscape | 420 × 297 | 4961 × 3508 |
| A1 Portrait | 594 × 841 | 7016 × 9933 |
| A1 Landscape | 841 × 594 | 9933 × 7016 |

Work at 300dpi for print output. For digital previews, a 1/3 or 1/4 downscale is plenty for review.

## Photo treatment

- Use `fit_image()` (crop-to-fill) rather than letterboxing. A letterboxed photo with grey bars never reads as TenneT.
- For portrait photos in landscape layouts: photo on left (~60%), text panel on right
- For landscape photos in portrait layouts: photo on top (~55%), text panel below
- Dark gradient overlays from bottom: gradient power 1.0–1.5, max alpha 230–240 (so text stays legible but the photo remains visible)
- Never apply color filters that clash with brand colors (no orange-tint filters, no heavy red/purple washes)

## Pattern A — Full Bleed + Gradient Bar

Photo fills the entire canvas. Dark blue gradient rises from the bottom (38–50% of the height). Text sits in the gradient area. Logo top-right with white backing. Pulse in the bottom corner.

**When to use:** Hero campaigns, event announcements, emotive subjects where the photo leads.

**Composition:**
- Full-bleed photo
- Dark blue gradient from bottom, 38–50% height, alpha 0→230
- Title in gradient area (UPPERCASE bold, white)
- Subtitle below title (light, white or light blue)
- Logo top-right with semi-transparent white backing
- Pulse in bottom-left corner, Bright Blue, 20% opacity

## Pattern B — Split Panel

Photo on one side, Dark Blue panel on the other. White logo and all text in the panel. Burst decoration in a panel corner.

**When to use:** Detailed information display — equipment posters, technical briefs, informational standees.

**Composition:**
- 55/45 or 60/40 split, photo on left (landscape) or top (portrait)
- Solid Dark Blue panel on the opposite side
- White logo in the panel, near the far corner from the photo
- All text in the panel: title, subtitle, function description, key points, accent line
- Burst overlay in the corner of the panel farthest from the logo, Bright Blue, 15–20%

## Pattern C — Full Bleed + Strong Gradient

Full photo with an aggressive bottom-to-top gradient. Green accents. White logo directly on the gradient (no backing needed). Burst overlay top corner.

**When to use:** Sustainability messaging, energy-transition themes.

**Composition:**
- Full-bleed photo
- Stronger gradient than Pattern A (60–70% of height, alpha 0→240)
- Green accent line between title and subtitle
- White logo on gradient, top-right, no backing
- Burst in top-left corner, Dark Blue, 18–22%

## Pattern D — White Frame (Museum Style)

White border around the photo. Text area below in white. Dark Blue typography. Orange pulse accent. Clean, premium feel.

**When to use:** Annual reports, high-end communications, award presentations.

**Composition:**
- White background with 5–8% margin on all sides
- Photo inside the frame, not full-bleed
- Text below the photo: title in Dark Blue bold, subtitle in muted text
- Orange accent line between photo and text area (2–4px, 8–15% of width)
- Pulse 1 in bottom-right, Orange, 30% opacity
- Color logo bottom-right inside the frame

## Pattern E — Header Band

Dark Blue header band at the top with title and white logo. Photo fills the middle section. Light footer with text and Green pulse accent. Orange line separating header from photo.

**When to use:** Informational posters where the title and body both need to be prominent (permits, station details, public information).

**Composition:**
- Dark Blue header band, 15–20% of height
- Title UPPERCASE white bold inside header, left-aligned
- White logo in header, top-right
- Orange line (2–4px) separating header from photo
- Photo middle section, 55–65% of height
- Near-white footer with body text and key points (Dark Text)
- Green pulse accent in footer, 25–30%

## Common element placement

Order of visual hierarchy from top to bottom within text areas:

1. Hero image or key visual (if not applying the full-bleed pattern)
2. Title (largest, boldest, often UPPERCASE)
3. Accent line (Orange or Green — never Blue)
4. Subtitle / description
5. "Function:" label + text
6. Key points (3 bullets)
7. Logo

## Accent lines

- Always Orange or Green — never Blue
- Always horizontal
- Width: 8–20% of container width (smaller in tighter layouts)
- Thickness: 0.2–0.4% of container width (so 2–4px on a 1000px-wide layout, 10–20px on a 5000px poster)
- Place directly after titles or as section dividers

## Grid and spacing

- Consistent margins: 5–8% of width on all sides
- Text-to-edge minimum padding: 6% of width
- Section spacing between blocks: 0.8–1.5× line height of the preceding block
